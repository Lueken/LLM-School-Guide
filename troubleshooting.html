Troubleshooting & Support

Solutions for common issues across all AI platforms

Platform-Specific Issues

When Claude Seems Confused

School Scenario:

"I asked Claude to help with my IEP goals, but it's giving me generic responses that don't fit my students."

Solutions:

  • • Add Specific Context: Include student grade level, disability category, current performance levels
  • • Provide Concrete Examples: Share previous IEP goals that worked well
  • • Break Down the Request: Ask for one goal at a time instead of entire IEP
  • • Use Education Terminology: Reference specific assessment tools, intervention programs
  • • Reset with Clarity: "Let's start over. I need help writing measurable IEP goals for a 3rd-grade student with autism who reads at kindergarten level."

When Gemini Returns Outdated Information

School Scenario:

"I asked about current special education law, but Gemini gave me information that seems outdated."

Solutions:

  • • Be Explicit About Timing: "Find information about special education regulations updated after January 2024"
  • • Request Source Dating: "Show me only sources from 2024-2025"
  • • Cross-Reference Multiple Sources: "Compare what different authoritative sources say about this"
  • • Specify Authority Level: "Focus on federal Department of Education sources and official state guidance"

When ChatGPT Free Cuts Off

School Scenario:

"ChatGPT stopped in the middle of generating my lesson plan."

Solutions:

  • • Immediate Recovery: "Continue from where you left off with the math center activities"
  • • Chunk Requests: Ask for lesson plan sections separately (objectives, activities, assessment)
  • • Save Partial Results: Copy good content before requesting more
  • • Switch Platforms: Move to Claude for complex lesson planning
  • • Time Strategy: Try during off-peak hours (early morning, late evening)

When Perplexity Sources Seem Weak

School Scenario:

"Perplexity is giving me blog posts instead of research when I ask about reading instruction."

Solutions:

  • • Specify Source Types: "Use only peer-reviewed research and government sources"
  • • Academic Language: Use terms like "evidence-based," "research-validated," "meta-analysis"
  • • Name Specific Journals: "Include sources from Reading Research Quarterly, Educational Psychology Review"
  • • Exclude Low-Quality Sources: "Do not include blog posts, social media, or commercial websites"

Team & Organizational Issues

Access Management Problems

License Conflicts

Problem: "Multiple staff members are trying to use the same shared account."

Solution:

  • • Audit current usage patterns to determine who needs dedicated access
  • • Calculate cost-benefit of individual vs. shared licenses
  • • Implement usage scheduling for shared accounts temporarily
  • • Request quotes for team/enterprise pricing

User Provisioning Delays

Problem: "New staff can't access AI tools they need for their work."

Solution:

  • • Create standard onboarding checklist including AI tool access
  • • Designate technology coordinator as AI account administrator
  • • Maintain list of approved tools and access levels by role
  • • Establish 48-hour turnaround goal for new user setup

Collaboration Challenges

Version Control Issues

"Different staff members working with different versions of AI-generated curriculum materials."

  • • Implement shared folder system
  • • Establish naming conventions
  • • Require check-out/check-in process
  • • Use collaborative editing

Handoff Communication Failures

"Context is lost when projects move between team members using different AI platforms."

  • • Standardize handoff template
  • • Require summary documentation
  • • Regular check-in meetings
  • • Create shared project logs

Performance & Cost Issues

Budget Overruns

Problem: "AI tool costs are exceeding department budgets."

Monthly Cost Management Process:

  1. 1. Usage Audit: Review which tools provide value vs. cost
  2. 2. User Analysis: Identify heavy users for team accounts
  3. 3. Task Assessment: Determine which tasks truly need paid features
  4. 4. Alternative Evaluation: Consider if free tools could handle some tasks
  5. 5. Negotiation: Contact vendors about education pricing

Inefficient Usage Patterns

Problem: "Staff using expensive tools for simple tasks."

Optimization Strategy:

  • • Provide role-specific training on platform selection
  • • Create decision trees for platform selection
  • • Monitor usage patterns and provide coaching
  • • Celebrate efficient usage in staff meetings

Compliance & Security Issues

Data Privacy Violations

Critical Problem: "Staff member accidentally included student PII in AI prompt."

Immediate Response Protocol:

  1. 1. Stop Usage: Immediately discontinue use of that account/platform
  2. 2. Document Incident: Record what information was shared and when
  3. 3. Contact Platform: Inquire about data deletion policies
  4. 4. Legal Review: Consult district legal counsel about notification requirements
  5. 5. Retraining: Provide additional privacy training to all staff

Policy Compliance Issues

Problem: "AI-generated content doesn't align with district policies or values."

Prevention and Response:

  • • Proactive: Create district-specific prompt templates that include policy alignment
  • • Review Process: Establish approval workflows for public-facing AI content
  • • Training: Regular workshops on creating prompts that reflect district values
  • • Quality Control: Random audit process for AI-assisted work outputs

Escalation Procedures

When to Move from Free to Paid Tiers

Individual Level Indicators:

  • • Using AI tools more than 2 hours per week
  • • Free tier limitations interrupt critical work
  • • Need for priority access during peak times
  • • Require advanced features for professional output

Department Level Indicators:

  • • Multiple team members hitting free tier limits
  • • Collaborative projects requiring shared access
  • • Need for enhanced security features
  • • ROI analysis shows productivity gains justify cost

Support Contact Priority

Before Contacting Support:

  • □ Check platform status pages for known issues
  • □ Review documentation and help resources
  • □ Try the issue on a different device/browser
  • □ Gather specific error messages and screenshots

Contact Priority:

  1. 1. Technical Issues: Platform-specific support channels
  2. 2. Billing/Account: Direct to platform billing support
  3. 3. Policy Questions: Internal IT/Legal team first
  4. 4. Training Needs: Internal professional development

Internal Team Support Structure

Level 1

Peer Support

Department colleagues with similar AI experience for informal mentoring and problem-solving

Level 2

Department Leaders

Department heads trained in advanced AI usage who can approve upgrades and connect with resources

Level 3

District Technology Team

IT coordinator with AI platform expertise for account access, security, and compliance issues

Level 4

External Consultants

Specialized AI in education consultants, vendor representatives, and legal counsel for complex issues
